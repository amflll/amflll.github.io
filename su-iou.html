The previous year's It's On US campaign focused solely on bystander intervention and the curriculum for facilitation sessions was a program developed in the University of New Hampshire. To revitalize the campaign and make it more relevant to WashU students, I co-chaired a focus group comprising of student advocates and educators of sexual assault prevention. I also reached out to various student groups to gather feedback on the program.

 

 

 

 

Results:

  • Expansion of the curriculum to include facilitation sessions on how to support a survivor and how the culture of sexual assault on campus is formed. Feedback from focus groups highlighted the lack of resources for friends of survivors and the desire to continue the conversations of sexual assault prevention beyond bystander intervention.
  • Incorporation of WashU specific case studies, gathered from existing educational materials and the student body, to make the takeaways from facilitation more applicable.
  • Streamlined the process of sign ups and feedback gathering by requiring all participants to fill up the respective forms online at the beginning and end of each facilitation session. The data was shared with the Relationship and Sexual Assault Prevention center for their record keeping and research purposes.
  • Passed the constitutional amendment that made the student group LIVE (Leaders in Interpersonal Violence Education) as a branch of Student Union to ensure the sustainability of the campaign and other efforts to end sexual assault on campus.
