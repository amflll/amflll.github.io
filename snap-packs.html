Food desert:

urban neighborhoods and rural towns without ready access to fresh, healthy, and affordable food.

 

The city of St. Louis faces numerous food deserts. Food banks supported by a network of organizations and private donors are currently the main resource for people living in a food desert. Recipients of food packages must often organize the various random items and find additional sources of fresh produce.

 

Snap Packs is a reusable packaging solution that can accommodate a wide variety of donated food products. Food banks will receive donations and pack them into a Snap Pack. Recipients will then exchange their empty Snap Pack from the previous week for a filled one. Donors can also choose to use Snap Packs, giving  food banks a unit of measurement to ensure the consistent distribution of produce. The physical exchange of the Snap Pack also catalyzes the formation of partnerships between food bank and recipient or food bank and donor, humanizing the distribution process.
