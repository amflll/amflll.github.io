Equity-Centered Community Design is a unique creative problem solving process based on equity, humility-building, integrating history and healing practices, addressing power dynamics, and co-creating with the community.

"Design your ideal neighborhood" is an activity that introduces participants to the process of participatory design and urban planning.

"Liberation by Design" is a card game or exercise used to teach participants about the definition of key terms such as: equity, equality, diversity, and inclusion.

Tackling systems of oppression with design.

 

Creative Reaction Lab is a St. Louis based nonprofit that works with Black and Latinx youth to co-create a healthy and racially equitable future for their communities.

 

Equity-Centered Community Design (ECCD) is a process that adapts principles of design thinking for historically marginalised communities.

 

As a Program and Education Associate (2017-2018) I created activities and tools to teach the ECCD process. This included the ECCD Field Guide that won a $30,000 grant from the 2017 Sappi Ideas that Matter program.
