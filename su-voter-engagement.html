Crucial to the democratic voting process is voter turnout. However voter turnout each semester is influenced by a range of factors such as competitiveness of the election, popularity of candidates, and nature of the candidate's platforms.

 

In the Spring of 2017,  the VP of Administration Cary Cheng and I worked together to break the voter turnout record. Our strategy focused on:

 

  • Educating students on how and when to vote
  • Making voting more accessible through organizing polling stations at popular dining locations
  • Learning from voter engagement campaigns, we created 'I Voted" to be distributed to students who voted at polling stations and a social media badge for those that voted on their personal computers.

 

Overall, the campaign was a success and set a new voter turnout record of 47% from 41%.
